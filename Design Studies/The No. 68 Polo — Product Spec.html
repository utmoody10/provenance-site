What the wearer sees

Collar Tape — Interior Face
Narrow woven tape bonded to inside face of collar. The amber/buff 1968 pattern — a direct reference to the card back. Width: 12mm. Jacquard woven.
Care Label Carrier + Woven Brand Label
The brand label sits on a carrier printed in the amber texture. Provenance wordmark in navy on cream — the P mark pattern as the field behind it. Interior centrepiece of the garment.
Placket Lining — Interior
The inside face of the button placket is lined with the cream-on-navy 1968 pattern — a finer scale than the collar, seen when the buttons are open. A discovery detail.
Back Detail Study

The No. 68 reference

The center back detail takes the card number badge format from the 1968 set and renders it as a small woven label at the base of the back collar. "68" in cream numerals on a navy field, enclosed in a rounded rectangle with a fine cream border — mirroring the proportion and layout of the original card number.

The reference is intimate and specific. It will not read as a sports reference to most viewers. To someone who knows the 1968 set, it is a direct quotation. To everyone else, it is a clean, elegant number detail — a finishing mark, like a hallmark on silver.

Placement: center back, 1.5cm below collar seam. Woven label, not embroidered. Size: 2.2cm wide × 1.6cm tall. Colorway: cream on navy, with fine cream border thread.

Material Specification

Fabric & construction

Component Specification Reference / Benchmark
Body Fabric Supima cotton 85% / TENCEL™ 15% piqué. 210 GSM. Two-ply. Enzyme washed, garment finished. Benchmark: Peter Millar Crown Sport piqué. Hand: soft, substantial, no sheen.
Collar + Cuffs Jacquard-woven 1968 mark pattern in amber (#C8823A) on buff ground (#E8C98A). Cotton/polyester blend for stability. 2×1 rib base with pattern woven in face. Custom fabric. Requires jacquard loom. Min. run: ~500m. Collar: 3.5cm spread. Cuffs: 2.5cm.
Placket Tape Woven tape in matching 1968 pattern. Bonded and stitched. Width: 2.8cm. 3 buttons, spaced at 4.5cm intervals. Same jacquard weave as collar. One fabric, two applications. Reduces tooling cost.
Buttons Natural horn, ecru. 18L (11mm). Flat 4-hole. Matte finish. No plastic. Horn picks up cream tone of body. Benchmark: Gitman Bros., Peter Millar linen shirts.
Interior Collar Tape Printed woven tape, amber/buff 1968 texture. 12mm width. Heat-bonded, then topstitched in matching thread. Single-needle. Screen print on twill tape, or digital print on satin tape. Budget: printed. Premium: narrow jacquard.
Brand Label Woven label, 4cm × 2.5cm. Provenance wordmark in navy on cream with pattern field. Centred, fold-under, sewn 3 sides. Jacquard woven label. Standard lead time: 6–8 weeks.
No. 68 Back Badge Woven label, 2.2cm × 1.6cm. "68" in Cormorant Garamond 300 numerals, cream on navy, fine cream border. Center back, base of collar. Woven or printed label. Discuss with label supplier — minimum at this size requires fine weave count.
P Mark Embroidery Left chest, 2.5cm height. Navy thread (Madeira: Polyneon 40 #1855 or match to #08121A). Chain stitch outline only. No fill. Density: 45°. Digitize from SVG. Outline-only reads more refined than filled embroidery at small scale.
Seam Finish French seam on side seams. Flat-felled on shoulder seams. Single-needle throughout. Peter Millar / Polo Ralph Lauren standard. No overlocked raw seams visible interior.
Fit Classic trim. Chest: 2.5cm ease over athletic fit. Hem: straight, 2cm below trouser waistband. Not a performance athletic cut — shaped but not compression. Benchmark: Peter Millar Seaside Polo, not Rhoback. Wearable from course to dinner.

What the design says

The No. 68 polo is a provenance object. Every detail is traceable — the border pattern to a specific print run, the number to a specific set, the fabric to a specific cotton variety. Nothing is arbitrary. This is the opposite of logo-driven luxury: it rewards knowledge rather than recognition. The person wearing it knows what it means. Most viewers will not. That asymmetry is the point.

What the design does not say

This is not a baseball polo. No team colors, no diamond motifs, no sport iconography. The 1968 reference is structural — drawn from the design language of the era, not its content. The collar pattern could as easily reference a vintage fabric sample card or a wallpaper record. The number 68 is not a jersey number. The design holds without explanation.
